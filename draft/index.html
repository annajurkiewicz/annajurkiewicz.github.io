About

Anna Jurkiewicz (she/her) engages in artistic research around human emotions and more-than-human timelines, architectures and interactions. Since 2019 she explores landscapes created by water through deep-time processes of dissolution and sedimentation, especially karst as a hydro-acoustic porous environment.

Many of her projects focus on multispecies relations that produce space and habitats. Her practice often intends to strengthen interspecies empathy by facilitating situations such as sensory walks, collective mapping, writing ecopoetry and performing participatory scores for interspecies choreographies of embodied cognition. She searches for engaging and multisensory forms of communicating artistic research, often including lecture performances, poetry and humour.

Her long-term research involves the underground, soil and soil-making organisms that recycle matter and facilitate growth and photosynthesis, such as springtails (the Silent Partners project, including video, scores, smells and poetry). As a sound artist she works with field recording, voice and found footage to create intimate multi-layered sound textures. As a musician she creates and recycles archives in ecofeminist contexts, composes, and explores resonance in various spaces, including an acoustic inventory of caves.

Her collaborations include the artistic duo Messy Sensing, the music duos ZYEM and Zaświatycze, and other projects. She explores the relational potential of resonance with groups in vocal workshops, often involving ritual songs, lamentation, and expanding the form of lullaby beyond the context of parenting.
